# VetRecover Women Veterans Digital Health Platform

> A 12-week AI-powered nutrition and wellness co-pilot designed for the 2.1M women veterans underserved by the VA healthcare system.

---

## Overview

VetRecover is a product strategy project developed at **NYU Tandon School of Engineering** as part of the *Design Strategies* course (MS Computer Engineering, 2025).

The project addresses a critical and largely invisible gap: the VA currently provides an average of **5 hours per year** of direct healthcare support per veteran, against an estimated **1,460 hours** of self-managed care needs annually. For women veterans a rapidly growing demographic this gap translates directly into poor nutrition outcomes, delayed recovery, and long-term health burdens.

VetRecover proposes a structured, personalized digital health product to bridge this gap through nutrition coaching, meal planning, and VA visit coordination.

---

## The Problem

Women veterans face a healthcare system built primarily around male service members. Key challenges include:

- **Nutritional neglect** standard VA diets and MRE-style rations are not designed for women's physiological needs
- **Self-care burden** veterans manage 1,460 hours of annual health decisions with minimal institutional support
- **Mental and physical overlap** trauma, medication interactions, and physical recovery are rarely addressed together
- **Access barriers** rural veterans, those with mobility issues, and those with PTSD face compounded difficulty accessing in-person care

---

## Proposed Solution

A **12-week digital co-pilot** built around four core product pillars:

| Pillar | Description |
|---|---|
| Personalized Meal Planning | Nutrition plans tailored to individual health history, service branch, and recovery goals |
| Trauma-Informed Coaching | Wellness guidance that accounts for PTSD, MST, and service-related mental health conditions |
| Medication-Food Coordination | Alerts and guidance on interactions between VA-prescribed medications and dietary choices |
| VA Visit Preparation | Structured pre-visit checklists and nutrition summaries to maximize VA appointment effectiveness |

## Go-To-Market Strategy

Primary acquisition channel: **VA Authorized Partner Program**

- Partner with VA health systems to offer VetRecover as a supplementary digital health tool
- Target veteran service organizations (VSOs) as distribution and trust-building networks
- Leverage telehealth infrastructure already in use post-COVID across VA facilities

---

## User Research

Conducted stakeholder interviews with women veterans to validate the core problem and solution hypotheses. Key insights:

- Veterans consistently reported feeling "on their own" between VA appointments
- Nutrition was identified as a high-priority but low-support area across all interview participants
- Digital tools were preferred over in-person group programs due to scheduling flexibility and privacy

---

## Stakeholder Map

- **Women Veterans** primary users; soldiers, sailors, and airforce personnel transitioning out of service
- **Military Doctors and Nutritionists** clinical validators and potential content partners
- **Defence Research Organizations (DRDO, USARIEM)** research backing for environment-specific nutrition needs
- **VA and Defence Ministries** procurement, authorization, and distribution partners
- **Food Technology Companies** potential suppliers for fortified and freeze-dried nutrition products
